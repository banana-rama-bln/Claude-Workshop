Das hier vorgestellte Pre-Assessment-Tool entstand aus einer doppelten Beobachtung: Erstens fehlt es im deutschsprachigen Raum an einer zugänglichen Erstabschätzung für Bestandsgebäude, die typologisch denkt, die richtige Sequenz von Sanierungsmaßnahmen abbildet und kritische Anschlussdetails sichtbar macht. ### EN – Long

**Profile**

I am an engineer and architect with two master's degrees and around seven years of professional experience at the intersection of sustainable planning, building physics, and heritage conservation. My work focuses on energy efficiency, resource-conscious construction, and responsible engagement with historic building substance – in both large-scale projects and detailed building-stock questions.

**Career**

After completing my Abitur and US High School Diploma at the John F. Kennedy School Berlin, I studied at Heriot-Watt University Edinburgh, earning my M.Eng. During my second master's, I worked as a research student at the Archdiocese of Berlin on a TU Berlin research project on data-driven optimisation of real estate management for ecclesiastical buildings.

At Tesla Manufacturing Brandenburg, from 2021 to 2025 I helped shape construction project management – starting as junior site manager, then as site manager handling 27 parallel new build and reconstruction projects during ongoing factory operations, and finally as architect and building physics planner across HOAI phases 1-6 with a focus on GEG compliance and BIM standardisation. Since May 2026, as Senior Project Manager at DB InfraGO AG, I have been responsible for the project development of passenger stations with a focus on sustainable usage concepts and heritage-compatible integration.

**About this project**

This pre-assessment tool grew from a twofold observation. First, the German-speaking market lacks an accessible initial assessment for existing buildings – one that thinks typologically, captures the correct sequence of renovation measures, and makes critical connection details visible. Second, I am experimenting with how current AI tools can be applied in building physics work – both to structure scattered data sources and to explore what future advisory tools might look like. The tool is showcase, study object, and conversation starter at once.
